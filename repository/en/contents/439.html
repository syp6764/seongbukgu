Household Waste Disposal Information

Disposals

All household waste must be disposed of in front of your home (at the entrance) according to the designated disposal dates.

warningPlease refrain from disposing of household waste on the day before public holidays, excluding Sundays.

Disposals

Disposals - Item, Type, Processing
Item Type Processing
General waste Tissue, gum, animal bones, tea bags, eggshells, fruit seeds, dried coffee grounds, herbal medicine (PAYT bags 20 liters or less) Use general PAYT bags
Food waste Fruits, vegetables, noodles, lean meat, etc. Use food waste PAYT bags(apartments: dedicated containers; small restaurants: proof of payment required)
Recyclable waste Paper, metal scraps, glass bottles, plastic Use transparent bags or tie with string for waste sorting
Other waste Inert waste, construction waste, building materials, glass ceramics, non-recyclable plastics Use special PAYT bags (available for purchase at volume-based PAYT bag sales locations)

Special PAYT Bag Waste Disposal

  • Inert waste
    Clothing, shoes, bags, hats, dolls, fabric, blankets, pillows, cushions, etc.

    warningReusable clothing and hats should be disposed of in nearby clothing collection bins.

  • Construction waste
    Concrete, bricks, tiles, roofing materials, etc.
  • Building materials
    Waste wallpaper, non-woven fabrics, wood, etc.
  • Glassware
    Glass, ceramic dishes, porcelain, vases, flower pots, aquariums, etc.
  • Non-recyclable plastics
    Wire insulation, conduits, PVC pipes, leather materials, etc.
  • Others
    Waste synthetic resins, waste leather, waste rubber, rubber hoses, various strings, rubber gloves, records, sponges, flooring materials, etc.
  • Non-recyclable items
    Incandescent bulbs, LED bulbs, fallen leaves, branches, cat litter, animal waste, gel ice packs, flower waste from florists, etc.

warningFor large items (e.g., blankets, dolls, glass, flooring materials, bags, wood, plastics, rubber bins, jars, rubber tubs, and aquariums), please report to The Bareun Hwangyeong before disposal.
